Learning Objective

Introduction

Recursion is a powerful technique in JavaScript that allows you to break down complex problems into smaller, more manageable pieces. Backtracking takes this concept even further, allowing you to explore multiple paths to find the optimal solution efficiently.

Duration: 8 Hours

Focus: Recursion & Back Tracking and their time and space complexity analysis

Pre-requisite: JavaScript

Theme

When a user watches a movie on Netflix or purchases a product on Amazon, the recommendation system needs to quickly and accurately suggest other movies or products that the user might be interested in. This requires analyzing vast amounts of data, including the user's viewing or purchase history, as well as other factors like demographics, reviews, and ratings.

Backtracking and recursion can be used to develop efficient algorithms for generating these recommendations. For instance, a recommendation algorithm may start by suggesting products or movies that are similar to what the user has already viewed or purchased, then recursively generate more suggestions based on the user's feedback.

Furthermore, these concepts can be used to optimize the recommendation algorithm by exploring different combinations of factors that may influence the user's preferences, such as genre, director, or cast. This process involves backtracking through the options to determine which combination of factors yields the best results.

Primary Goals

  • Gain comprehension of recursion and backtracking, and their practical applications in problem-solving.
  • Grasp the trade-offs involved in choosing between recursion and iteration, and discern when each approach is preferable.
  • Develop the ability to analyze the time and space complexity of recursive and backtracking algorithms.
  • Acquire knowledge of the real-world implementation of recursion and backtracking in various applications.
